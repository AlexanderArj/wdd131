Tallinn, Estonia

A great place to visit...

Last July, I had the opportunity to visit this great little country! The city of Tallinn is beautiful, straight out of a medieval fantasy story. I was able to visit some of the city's museums and walk its cobblestone streets; it was simply spectacular.

Weather

The tourist season in Tallinn is summer, during the months of June, July, and August. This city tends to have very low temperatures in winter, so if you don't like the cold, it's best to plan a trip during the summer.

Tallinn, one of the best-preserved medieval cities in Europe...

Tallinn is a beautiful and ancient city in Estonia. Its most special part is the Old Town, one of the best-preserved medieval cities in Europe. The streets are made of ancient cobblestones, and you can see colorful houses, tall church towers, and ancient walls. You can visit the main square, admire the ancient walls, or simply get lost in its small, beautiful streets. This is truly an unforgettable place.

Beyond its medieval charm, Tallinn is also a modern city with vibrant cafes, innovative museums, and a lively arts scene.
The city’s seaside promenades offer stunning views of the Baltic Sea, and its parks provide peaceful spots to relax.
Whether you are interested in history, culture, or simply enjoying beautiful scenery, Tallinn has something for everyone.

Tallinn's Culinary...

For a truly authentic culinary experience in Tallinn, a visit to the Balti Jaama Turg market is a must.

Here, you can discover a variety of local flavors. The market also has many food stalls where you can try traditional marinated herring and rye bread, along with modern Estonian dishes and international foods. It is the perfect spot to meet locals, find unique ingredients, and enjoy a delicious and affordable meal.
