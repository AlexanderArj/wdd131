Amsterdam, Netherlands

Amazing place...

Amsterdam is the largest city in the Netherlands and a cultural, historical, and commercial center. It is known for its canal system, artistic heritage, and narrow, gabled houses.

Weather

Amsterdam has a mild climate. Summers are cool and winters are mild. While it rains throughout the year, the best time to visit is from April to September. During these months, the weather is more pleasant with less rain and more sun, making it ideal for exploring the city's canals and attractions.

Amsterdam: The City of Canals...

With over 100 kilometers of canals, Amsterdam is often called the "Venice of the North." These canals, along with their historic bridges and houses, are a UNESCO World Heritage site. A unique feature of the city are the "grachtenhuizen," 17th-century canal houses that often lean forward, adding to Amsterdam's charm.

Dutch Cuisine...

In Amsterdam, be sure to try the famous pannenkoeken, which are Dutch pancakes. They are much larger and thinner than American pancakes, similar to a crêpe, and can be ordered with either sweet or savory fillings. If you're looking for something smaller, try the poffertjes, which are fluffy mini-pancakes served with melted butter and powdered sugar, perfect for a quick snack.
